I. The primary rules
   1.  Non-Wiccan Doctrine
       a. This tradition was not founded on any version of modern pagan practice and has no association to any other or any known Religion.
    
   2. Gaia Doctrine
      a. Gaia, Mother Earth, the living planet we live with, not upon, is the Goddess, Queen of all kind and all creation.
      b. From her we come, and to her we return. (Ashes to ashes, and dust to dust)
     
   3. Doctrine of Sovereignty
      a. Each member is responsible for their own path, their own conduct, and their own development.
      b. Rosaic does not exist to create dependency, obedience, or submission.
     
   4. Doctrine of Silence
      a. The Circle is not a performance.
         (1) Noise is unnecessary.
         (2) Speech is minimal.
         (3) Work is done in silence unless instruction is required.
         
   5. Doctrine of KISS
      a. Rosaic follows the KISS doctrine: Keep It Stupid Simple.
      b. Complexity for appearance is forbidden.
      c. Authenticity requires restraint.
     
   6. Doctrine of the Bell
      a. Circle activity is governed by the Keeper's Bell.
      b. The bell marks gathering, casting, invocation, working, closing, and dismissal.
 
   7. Doctrine of the Elements
      a. The Rosaic Tradition only recognizes 4 elements:
         (1) Fire
         (2) Water
         (3) Earth
         (4) Air
      b.  Spirit is not treated as an element, as it is individual and not a station of nature.
    
   8. Doctrine of Casting and Closing
      a. The Circle is cast by clockwise procession.
      b. The Circle is dismantled by counter-clockwise procession.
      c. The Prioress enters and exits last.
 
   9. Doctrine of Stations
      a. Each Circle shall have stations of authority:
         (1) Prioress
         (2) Priestesses of the Elements
         (3) Keeper
         (4) Squire

      b. These roles are functional, not ceremonial.
    
   10. Doctrine of Rank Restriction
       a. Circle activity shall be restricted to Priestess rank or higher.
     
   11. Doctrine of Yielded Authority
       a. Should a higher-ranked member exist within a Coven, they shall yield authority to the Coven’s Prioress.
     
   12. Doctrine of Thirteen
       a. Each Coven shall be no more and no less than thirteen (13) in number.
       b. Seekers do not count
 
   13. Doctrine of the Lower Grades
       a. Each Coven shall have no more than six (6) total members of the Neophyte and Initiate grades combined.
 
   14. Doctrine of Time
       a. Progression is earned through time and discipline.
       b. A year and a day is the standard measure of development.
 
   15. Doctrine of Provisional Covens
       a. Provisional Covens exist for the purpose of developing a traditional Rosaic Coven.
       b. A provisional coven has a year and a day to prove itself before recognition is granted.
     
   16. Doctrine of Continuity
       a. The Squire shall maintain written minutes and records for continuity.
       b. Records (Rolls) are permanent.
 
   17. Doctrine of No Politics
       a. Politics are forbidden within Rosaic structure.
       b. The Circle is not a democracy.
       c. Ego-driven debate is corruption.
 
   18. Doctrine of Restraint in Authority
       a. Authority must be restrained.
       b. Power from position begets corruption.
       c. Corruption becomes politics.
       d. Politics must not be allowed.
     
   19. Doctrine of Written Language
       a. Rosaic maintains written language to preserve identity, continuity, and controlled transmission of doctrine.
     
   20. Doctrine of Modern Knowledge
       a. Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics are essential.
       b. Alchemy is recognized as the ancient root of chemistry.
       c. Rosaic does not reject science, but rejects the belief that all things are explainable by science.
     
  21. Doctrine of the True Year
      a. The natural year begins in renewal, not in arbitrary human decree.
      b. The cycles of Luna and the seasonal shift of Earth are the proper measures of time.
    
  22. Doctrine of Offerings
      a. Herbology and alchemy are sacred acts.
      b. Harvest requires acknowledgment.
      c. Gratitude is required.
    
  23. Doctrine of Kali
      a. Kali strips away what is false to reveal what is true.
      b. Thus Rosaic rejects performance, vanity, and artificiality.
    
  24. Doctrine of Hekate
      a. Hekate is recognized as the Keeper of thresholds.
      b. She governs:
         (1) crossroads
         (2) gates
         (3) transitions
         (4) the unseen boundary between what is known and what is hidden
      c. In Rosaic, Hekate represents discipline in passage.
      d. No seeker crosses into deeper knowledge without preparation, restraint, and consequence.
      e. Hekate is not invoked for spectacle, nor for ego.
      f. She is acknowledged when entering a new grade, a new oath, or a new burden.
         (1) The path is not opened by desire.
         (2) It is opened by worth.
       
   25. Doctrine of Hedone
       a. Hedone is recognized as the force of pleasure and sweetness.
       b. She governs:
          (1) joy
          (2) desire
          (3) indulgence
          (4) the intoxication of comfort
       c. In Rosaic, Hedone represents the reminder that life is meant to be lived, not merely endured.
       d. But Hedone is also a test.
          (1) Pleasure without discipline becomes addiction.
          (2) Joy without restraint becomes corruption.
          (3) Comfort without awareness becomes decay.
       e. Hedone is honored as a gift of Gaia, but never allowed to become a master.
          (1) A witch may enjoy.
          (2) A witch must never be owned.
          
   26. Doctrine of Title
       a. Modern day ranking structures are prohibited.
       b. Titles such as High-Priestess are Arch-Anything will never be allowed.
       c. These titles derive from Wicca and modern Neo-Pagan Movements.

III. Succession

     1. Succession is automatic, not elected
        a. No popularity contests. No voting. No campaigns.
        b. Vacancies are filled by rank + qualification.

     2. Seniority matters, but competence overrides
        a. Default successor = most senior qualified in that office’s grade range.
        b. But the Madam/Office can override if the person is unstable, unreliable, or corruptible.

     3. Succession requires a “quiet confirmation”
        a. Not a ceremony. Not an announcement.
        b. A private acknowledgment by the outgoing officer (or the overseeing office).

     4. Acting appointments are allowed
        a. If the successor is not ready, an Acting role can be assigned for a defined time period (ex: 90 days).

     5. Removal doctrine must exist
        a. If an officer becomes political, abusive, or negligent, they are removed without drama.

     6. The Keeper and Squire protect continuity
        a. Succession is meaningless without records.
        b. The Squire ensures doctrine and minutes transfer cleanly.

VI. Bell Method
   1. The bell method is done in practical silence and is the core of every festival's beginning and end.

   2. A Rosaic Circle is conducted by the following stations:
      a. Prioress
      b. Elemental Priestesses
         (1) Fire
         (2) Water
         (3) Earth
         (4) Wind
      c. Keeper
      d. Squire
   3. Opening the Circle
      a. On one (1) toll of the Keeper's bell with a vocal announcement
         (1) All circle members move to the circles location.
      b. The Eastern Assembly, four (4) tolls of the Keepers Bell
         (1) Circle members move to the most eastern point of the circle boundary.
         (2) Circle members line up in order
             (a) Fire
             (b) Water
             (c) Earth
             (d) Wind
             (e) The Prioress moves next to the entrance
      c. Enter ye circle upon three (3) tolls of the keepers bell
         (1) The Prioress takes the hand of each Elemental Priestess and guides them into the circle.
      d. The Element Priestesses proceed clockwise to their stations in the following order:
         (1) Fire
             (a) Aries
             (b) Leo
             (c) Sagittarius
         (2) Water
             (a) Scorpio
             (b) Cancer
             (c) Pisces
         (3) Earth
             (a) Taurus
             (b) Virgo
             (c) Capricorn
         (4) Wind
             (a) Gemini
             (b) Aquarius
             (c) Libra
      e. As each Element Priestess walks to her station, she holds her hand, wand, or blade toward the outer rim of the Circle, focusing energy into the boundary, ceasing only upon reaching her destination.
        (1) Thus the circle is cast by motion and intent.
      f. Calling the corners on two (2) tolls of the keeper's bell
         (1) The Prioress faces to the east and slightly bows slightly to the Elemental Priestess
         (2) The Element Priestess in the East turns fully outward and silently invokes the element of Air.
             (a) A candle and incense pertaining to the element may be lit.
         (3) Upon completion, the Element Priestess turns back inward, returns her gaze to the Prioress, and bows slightly.
         (4) This procession continues around the circle until all four elements have been invoked.
         (5) Spirit is not called as an element, as it is individual and not a station of nature.
      g. This invocation is led strictly by the Prioress with no interaction from the Keeper's bell. 
   4. Raising of Energy
      a. A slow, low rhythmic chant begins to raise the energy of the Circle.
      b. This is to provide the needed energy for the work ahead
   5. The work at one (1) toll of the Keeper's bell.
      a. The coven goes about it work in relevant silence
     
   6. The Keeper maintains the timeline
      a. Using a pre determined schedule.
   7. The work ends, on one (1) toll of the Keepers bell:
      a. The intent of the work is complete
   8. Do it in reverse on two (2) tolls of the keepers bell:
      a. The Prioress faces to the east and bows slightly to the Elemental Priestess
      b. The Elemental Priestess turn to face outward and:
         (1) releases the invoked element
         (2) quenches any candle or incense
         (3) turn inward
         (4) a slight bow to the Prioress
     c. This procession continues around the circle until all elements have been released
   9. Let us leave, on two (2) tolls of the Keeper's bell.
      a. The Prioress moves in sequence:
         (1) South
         (2) West
         (3) North
         (4) East
             (a) bowing slightly to each and gesturing them to move proceed counter-clockwise to the east
      b. Exit procession follows:
         (1) Fire
         (2) Water
         (3) Earth
         (4) Wind
         (5) the Prioress
      c. As they walk counter-clockwise, the Circle is deconstructed.
      d. All members gather at the East outside the Circle boundary until all have exited.
  10. Dismissal
      a. On 3 tolls of the Keepers bell:
         (1) The member may move away from the circle
  11. Complete
      a. on one (1) toll from the Keepers bell:
         (1) Circle members depart
         (2) Break down begins by support people

XI. Ogham
    1. Ogham is an early Irish script of twenty primary characters, used historically as both an alphabet and a structured system of marking. It is divided into four groupings known as the Aicme (Ack-mee), each containing five characters.
       a. Beithe (Be eth)- The Aicme of Beginning
       b. hÚatha (Hu atha) - The Aicme of Testing
       c. Muine (Moo in) - Expansion
       d. Ailme (Ale mee) - Completion
    2. In Rosaic teaching, Ogham is treated as a discipline of perception—a structured method of reading nature, self, and time. It is not used as superstition, entertainment, or aesthetic mimicry, but as a symbolic framework for progression.
       a. The Aicme define the season.
       b. Not merely in weather, but in the human condition.
       c. Kennings, word-poems, will not be used as the are widely open to interpretation.
    3. The Doctrine of Aicme
       a. The Aicme are not merely letter groupings.
       b. They represent a natural order:
          (1) Beginning
          (2) Testing
          (3) Expansion
          (4) Completion
       c. All things pass through these phases.
       d. The seasons do not ask permission.
       e. The soul does not escape this cycle.
       f. Thus, in Rosaic teaching, the Aicme are used as a framework to measure:
          (1) Growth of the self
          (2) Maturity of a coven
          (3) Readiness for rank
          (4) Stability of identity

    2. The Equinoxes
       a. The in between, that exist between the major four festivals.
          (1) Eostara ( Ee oh Star Ah)
              a. On or around March 21, the beginning of Spring.
          (2) Litha (Lee tha)

          (3) Mabon (May Bon)
